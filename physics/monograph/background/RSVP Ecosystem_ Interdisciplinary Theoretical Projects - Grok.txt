The blank pages are from two book-class defaults and one package conflict:
üîç Causes
\documentclass{book}
The book class always:
starts each chapter on a right-hand (odd-numbered) page,
and inserts a blank left-hand (even-numbered) page before it when needed.
That's why you see extra blanks.
\usepackage[empty]{fullpage} + geometry
fullpage (especially with [empty]) resets margins and page style, conflicting with geometry.
It can also suppress running headers/footers by forcing \cleardoublepage behavior to output blank pages.
\pagestyle{empty}
Removes page numbers (fine), but combined with book + cleardoublepage, you get totally blank filler pages‚Äîno header, no footer, no numbers.
‚úÖ Solutions (choose one depending on what you want)
Option A ‚Äî Keep the book structure but remove blank pages
Add this before \begin{document}:
\let\cleardoublepage\clearpage
This tells LaTeX: "don't insert blank left-hand pages between chapters."
You'll keep the proper chapter formatting but no blanks.
Option B ‚Äî Switch to report class
If you don't need two-page book printing (recto-verso layout):
\documentclass[12pt]{report}
The layout and numbering stay identical, but no blank pages ever appear.
You can still use \chapter, \section, etc.
Option C ‚Äî Keep book class but disable fancy page style safely
Replace both fullpage and \pagestyle{empty} with this cleaner setup:
\documentclass[12pt]{book}
\usepackage[a4paper,margin=1in]{geometry}
\usepackage{amsmath,amssymb,amsthm}
\usepackage{graphicx}
\usepackage{hyperref}
\usepackage{listings}
\usepackage{xcolor}
\usepackage{enumitem}
\usepackage{float}
\pagestyle{plain} % or 'empty' if you prefer no numbers
\let\cleardoublepage\clearpage % avoid blank pages before chapters
This removes the fullpage conflict, keeps consistent margins, and stops chapter breaks from forcing empties.
