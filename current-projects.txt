### 1. Relevance Field
The Relevance Field is a scalar function defined over perceptual or action space, encapsulating the relevance of stimuli under given context:

    $$
    R: \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R}, \quad x \mapsto R(x)
    $$

    Here, $\mathcal{X}$ represents the perceptual/spatial manifold (e.g., visual field or body space), and $R(x)$ quantifies the relevance of stimulus $x$.

### 2. Cue-Driven Activation Function
A cue (e.g., smell, sight, sound) activates a region in this field:

    $$
    A_c(x) = \phi(\|x - x_c\|) \cdot w_c
    $$

    where $x_c$ is the cue-indexed region, $\phi$ is a kernel function (e.g., Gaussian), and $w_c$ is the salience weight of that cue.

### 3. Gradient Dynamics
Behavior or attention follows the gradient of relevance:

    $$
    \frac{dx}{dt} = \nabla R(x)
    $$

    More generally, behavior can be influenced by internal modulator parameters (e.g., arousal, stress level):

    $$
    \frac{dx}{dt} = f(R(x), \nabla R(x), \theta)
    $$

### 4. Hebbian Trail Formation
Synaptic/path reinforcement follows a Hebb-like rule:

    $$
    \Delta w_{ij} = \eta \cdot A_i \cdot A_j
    $$

    Here, $A_i$, $A_j$ are activations of relevance units $i$, $j$, and $\eta$ is the learning rate.

### 5. Place Field Encoding
Model hippocampal-like place encoding:

    $$
    \psi_i(x) = e^{-\frac{\|x - \mu_i\|^2}{2\sigma^2}}, \quad R(x) = \sum_i \alpha_i \psi_i(x)
    $$

    Each $\psi_i$ represents a localized relevance "bump" (e.g., place cell), and $R(x)$ is the sum of these bumps weighted by factors $\alpha_i$.
