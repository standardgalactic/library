# Briefing on Event-Historical Frameworks for Meaning, Agency, and Power

## Executive Summary

This document synthesizes a series of interconnected frameworks advancing a unified thesis: **meaning, agency, power, and physical reality are constituted by irreversible, history-dependent events rather than mutable states**. A system's identity is defined by the futures it has permanently ruled out.

At the core is the **Spherepop calculus**, a formal language in which primitive operations—**pop, bind, refuse, collapse**—act directly on spaces of admissible futures. This calculus is complemented by:

- **Constraint-first semantics**, explaining symbolic conflict as reification errors.
- **A critique of autoregressive AI**, arguing that systems without irreversible commitment lack agency.
- **A structural theory of social power**, modeling *mute compulsion* as survival-aligned constraint.
- **A physical ontology (RSVP)** in which spacetime and quantum formalisms emerge from lossy compression of an irreversible, non-Markovian plenum.

Across logic, computation, society, and physics, **worlds are built by irreversible commitments**.
