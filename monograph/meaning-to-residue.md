# From Meaning to Residue: A Mechanistic Breakdown of Information Decay in the Feed

In contemporary information systems, social media feeds are often misinterpreted as neutral distribution channels. In reality, they function as active selection apparatuses that transform the information they carry. This transformation is governed by the inverse relationship between an idea’s internal constraints and its ability to propagate.

To understand this decay, we must formalize the tension between binding force and semantic portability.

---

## 1. The fundamental conflict: Binding force vs recomposability

Every informational unit exists in tension between two mutually exclusive properties: how strongly it enforces its own meaning, and how easily it can be displaced across contexts.

Binding force φ measures the degree to which an idea remains identical to itself across transformations. Its goal is semantic invariance. Examples include mathematical proofs or strictly defined technical specifications.

Recomposability ρ measures how easily an idea can be clipped, quoted, and reinserted into new contexts. Its goal is propagation velocity. Examples include slogans, reaction images, and affectively charged fragments.

### The inverse relationship

These properties are structurally inverse:

ρ(c) ≈ 1 − φ(c)

Binding force is inversely related to semantic sensitivity σ(c), which measures how much meaning shifts under context changes.

High recomposability increases sensitivity and reduces the capacity of an idea to carry stable meaning.

### Learning narrative

Feeds are not neutral channels. They act as filters that penalize high-binding content.

To survive, ideas must reduce internal constraint and increase portability.

---

## 2. The selection functional: Formalizing platform preferences

Content distribution is governed by a selection functional J_platform.

This functional optimizes three variables:

Engagement E, measuring attention and interaction  
Recomposability ρ, measuring portability across contexts  
Cognitive cost K, measuring effort required for consumption  

### System behavior

The system shifts cognitive cost onto creators and early adopters.

High-binding, high-cost content is structurally penalized.

As a result, perceived depth is often simulated rather than real.

### Definition: Pseudo-binding

Pseudo-binding is the simulation of constraint through aesthetic or rhetorical markers while preserving recomposability.

Examples include technical language without precision, slow pacing without structure, and tonal seriousness without logical commitment.

---

## 3. The drift theorem: The mechanics of semantic decay

Information designed for propagation undergoes a cycle of decay.

### Lifecycle of an idea

Creation begins with high semantic density and binding.

Recomposition increases recomposability by clipping or simplifying.

Context-switching embeds the idea into new environments.

Semantic loss occurs as meaning fails to bind across contexts.

### Formal result

lim (n → ∞) φₙ(c) = 0

Binding force converges to zero through repeated recomposition.

### Intuition

An idea behaves like a stone in a river. Repeated collisions smooth its features.

At maximum portability, the idea becomes a featureless semantic residue.

High virality corresponds to high instability of meaning.

---

## 4. The Trojan horse failure

The Trojan horse strategy attempts to embed complex ideas within accessible formats.

This fails because recomposition removes inferential structure.

### Structural outcome

The audience receives conclusions without the reasoning required to validate them.

The result is recomposable residue: emotionally charged fragments detached from logic.

### Critical observation

Inferential structure is the least portable component of an idea.

Because it cannot be clipped without destruction, it is systematically removed.

---

## 5. Adaptation as convergence: The aesthetic filter

Creators adapt to selection pressure by minimizing binding force while maintaining visibility.

### Dominant strategies

Aestheticization reduces cognitive cost through visual and tonal cues.

Narrativization replaces logical structure with emotional arcs.

### Structural consequence

Maintaining pseudo-binding requires production resources.

This creates a privilege structure where only resource-rich creators can simulate depth at scale.

### Cost of survival

To achieve visibility, creators sacrifice:

Precision in definitions  
Nuanced qualification  
Logical structure  
Inferential transparency  
Internal binding  

Meaning is replaced by persona and presentation.

---

## 6. Conclusion: The great bifurcation

Information systems split into two incompatible regimes.

The selection regime optimizes propagation and recomposability.

The closure regime optimizes semantic invariance and correctness.

### Formal contrast

Selection regime is governed by J_platform and maximizes E and ρ.

Closure regime is governed by J_closure:

J_closure(c) = λ · φ(c) − μ · δ(c)

Closure rewards binding force and penalizes inconsistency.

### Structural incompatibility

Optimization in one regime degrades performance in the other.

High propagation requires loss of binding.

High binding requires withdrawal from propagation.

---

## Final synthesis

Information decay is not accidental.

It is the direct consequence of selection pressure.

Meaning survives only where recomposability is resisted.

Maximum reach and maximum meaning cannot coexist.
